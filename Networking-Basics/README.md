# 🌐 Networking Fundamentals
This section documents my foundational knowledge in networking, which is the backbone of Cybersecurity and Purple Teaming.

## 🏗️ Network Classifications
I have studied the primary types of network architectures based on geographical scale:

* **LAN (Local Area Network):** High-speed network covering a small area like a home or office.
* **MAN (Metropolitan Area Network):** Connects multiple LANs across a city-wide area.
* **WAN (Wide Area Network):** Covers large distances (countries or continents), with the Internet being the largest example.

## 🔌 Physical Layer & Cabling
Understanding the physical medium is essential for network security and troubleshooting. I focused on Twisted Pair cables:

### 1. UTP (Unshielded Twisted Pair)
* The most common cable used in LANs.
* Flexible and cost-effective but susceptible to electromagnetic interference (EMI).

### 2. STP (Shielded Twisted Pair)
* Contains extra shielding (foil or braided mesh) to protect signals from interference.
* Used in industrial environments or areas with high electrical noise.

## 📐 Network Topologies
I explored how devices are connected logically and physically:
* **Star Topology:** Most common in modern LANs where all devices connect to a central switch.
* **Mesh Topology:** Provides high redundancy, crucial for critical infrastructure.
